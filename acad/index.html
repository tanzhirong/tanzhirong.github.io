I graduated from the University of Chicago with an M.S. in Statistics in August 2019, advised by Professor Lek-Heng Lim. I am interested in statistical machine learning, specifically unsupervised learning methods, and their interpretation in the Social Sciences. My Master's thesis focuses on the application of nonlinear dimensionality reduction and kernel methods in unsupervised learning techniques, to understand and mathematically model the voting patterns of multi-party legislative bodies.

I received my B.S. in Mathematics (Specialization in Economics), similarly from the University of Chicago, in June 2018.
